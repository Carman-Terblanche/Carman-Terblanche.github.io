Digital Bank

My Role

I was given a task to solve a problem where a fictitious bank wanted to imcrease the users for their mobile app.

My Approach

In order to do proper research, I first needed to understand the problem better. We know that the new banking app for a digital bank isn’t making any traction, so I had to ask myself a few questions: Who will be interested in the app? Why is the current campaign unsuccessful? I had to identify the category of app in this scenario. We know that people generally don’t want to bloat their devices with every new app that comes to the play store / app store, however, there is a distinction. Functional and Leisure apps. I don't use my banking app because I enjoy it, but because there is a functional reason to do so. Since I have an account with, say, Bank X, I have incentive to get the app as it makes my life a lot easier. There is a function. With this information, I now had to approach how to get useful research. Marketing isn’t necessarily the problem, as we understand that anyone who is part of the bank will have incentive to download it. However, there is probably a demographic that thinks otherwise. So I can ask that demographic why they don’t use their bank’s banking app. I headed to Reddit and Twitter for some user feedback. What I learned

I went into this project thinking the answer was simple. The problem seemed simple enough to understand that I thought the solution would also be. Little did I know that what's on the surface hardly ever reflects the scope that you're dealing with. When the team and I began unpacking all the aspects of the project, including the different scenarios each user could make, we started to realise that we had underestimated the scope.

We strapped ourselves in to take apart each problem and find a solution. The efforts paid off in the end, but the journey was a rollercoaster.
